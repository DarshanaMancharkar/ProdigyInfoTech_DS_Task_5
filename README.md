### Task 5: Traffic Accident Pattern Analysis

## 📖 Project Overview

This project analyzes the **Road Traffic Accidents (RTA) dataset** to identify meaningful patterns related to:
- 🛣️ Road conditions  
- 🌦️ Weather conditions  
- ⏰ Time of day  

The goal is to understand accident trends, visualize accident hotspots, and identify contributing factors that influence accident severity.

## 🎯 Objectives

- Perform exploratory data analysis (EDA) on traffic accident data  
- Analyze accident patterns based on:
  - Time of day
  - Weather conditions
  - Road surface conditions
  - Light conditions
- Visualize accident severity across different factors
- Identify high-risk conditions and trends

## 📂 Dataset Description

The RTA dataset contains detailed information about road traffic accidents, including:

- Time and day of accident  
- Driver demographics  
- Vehicle details  
- Road and weather conditions  
- Accident severity and casualties  

📌 Dataset used as provided by Prodigy Infotech for internship tasks.

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used

- **Python**
- **Jupyter Notebook**
- **Pandas** – Data manipulation
- **NumPy** – Numerical operations
- **Matplotlib & Seaborn** – Data visualization

## 📊 Analysis & Visualizations

The following analyses were performed:

- Accidents distribution by **time of day**
- Impact of **weather conditions** on accidents
- Effect of **road surface conditions**
- Relationship between **light conditions and accident severity**
- Accident severity vs **number of vehicles involved**
- Categorization of accidents into:
  - Single-vehicle accidents
  - Two-vehicle collisions
  - Multi-vehicle accidents


## 🔍 Key Insights

- Accident frequency peaks during **rush hours**
- Most accidents occur during **clear weather**, indicating human factors play a major role
- **Dry road surfaces** show higher accident counts due to higher vehicle speeds
- Poor **lighting conditions** increase accident severity
- **Multi-vehicle accidents** tend to be more severe than single-vehicle accidents

## 📊 Results

The analysis shows that traffic accidents peak during rush hours, indicating congestion as a major factor. Most accidents occur under clear weather and dry road conditions, highlighting the impact of driver behavior. Poor lighting conditions increase accident severity, and multi-vehicle accidents tend to be more severe than single-vehicle crashes.

## 🧠 Conclusion

The analysis shows that traffic accidents are strongly influenced by **time of day, traffic density, and driving behavior**, rather than weather alone.  
Improving **traffic control during peak hours**, **road lighting**, and **speed enforcement** can significantly reduce accident severity and frequency.
